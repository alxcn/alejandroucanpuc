# Hello, I'm Alejandro!

I'm currently a full-time professor at Tecnologico de Monterrey, Campus Monterrey since August 2022. I'm part of the Mathematics Department and I teach courses exploring courses for the Engineering programs, as well as specialized courses in the Data Science and Mathematics programs.

I hold a Ph.D. from the Instituto de Matemáticas de la UNAM, where I worked with Dr. José Seade and Dr. Angel Cano, with a research about Dynamics and Geometry of discrete subgroups of the projective complex linear group. My research interests include discrete groups dynamics, geometric structures, topological data analysis, and artificial intelligence.

Previously, I was at the Institut de Mathématiques de Jussieu Paris-Rive Gauche in a Postdoctoral fellowship with Dr. Elisha Falbel, where I worked on the uniformization of orbifolds and representation varieties. 

I have published papers related to limit sets of projective discrete groups, uniformization of orbifolds, representation varieties, geometric structures, and computational algorithms for Hausdorff dimensions. Currently, I'm interested in the detection of LLM-generated text using topological features and Reidemaister moves using AI.

I'm part of the leaders of the Mathematics competition team at Tecnologico de Monterrey, where we participate in national and international competitions, like the Mexican Unversitary Mathematics Olympiad (OMUM), the Ibero-American Interuniversity Mathematics Competition (CIIM), the Putnam Competition, etc.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I have a variety of personal interests. I enjoy to watch anime, going to the gym, play video games and listening to pop music, especially French pop. I also have a passion for baking, and I love to experiment with new recipes in the kitchen.

If you have any questions or would like to connect, feel free to reach out to me via email at [alejandro.ucan-puc@tec.mx](mail_to:alejandro.ucan-puc@tec.mx)
